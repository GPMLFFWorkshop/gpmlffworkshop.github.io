1st Edition of the General-purpose Machine-Learned Force Fields in Theory and Practice

This workshop brings together researchers developing and applying general-purpose machine-learned force fields for atomistic simulations. Through lectures and hands-on tutorials covering theory, symmetry-aware modeling, and practical deployment, it aims to foster critical understanding and strengthen the emerging community of users and developers.

About the Workshop

About the Workshop

The workshop convenes researchers advancing general-purpose machine-learned force fields and their application to atomistic simulations in chemistry and materials science. Addressing the theoretical principles underlying symmetry and equivariance, the practical challenges of training and validation, and the deployment of robust models in research workflows, the program combines lectures, tutorials, and discussion sessions. The event seeks to promote critical understanding, broaden adoption, and build a collaborative community around next-generation data-driven force fields.
